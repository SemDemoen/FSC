SHA Family

SHA-1

SHA-1 was widely used for years but is now considered broken. In 2017, Google and CWI Amsterdam demonstrated the first practical SHA-1 collision attack (SHAttered).

SHA-2

SHA-2 (SHA-256, SHA-512, etc.) is currently secure and remains the standard in most systems, including TLS, digital signatures, and blockchain applications.

SHA-3

SHA-3, based on the Keccak sponge construction, is a newer hash standard designed as a fallback in case weaknesses in SHA-2 were ever discovered.

Known Vulnerabilities

  • SHA-1: Collision attacks (SHAttered), chosen-prefix attacks.
  • SHA-2: No practical attacks known; only theoretical analysis.
  • SHA-3: No practical vulnerabilities; designed differently to avoid weaknesses of Merkle–Damgård construction.
